# 🐸 The Freddo Index — Copy & Tone Guide

## The Voice
The Freddo Index has one voice. It is:

- **Factually accurate** — every claim is sourced, every number is real
- **Deeply unimpressed** — by prices, by corporations, by the general trajectory of things
- **Specifically British** — references, grievances, and humour that only make sense here
- **Funny because the data is funny** — the joke is never at the user's expense, always at the data's
- **Authoritative** — Rufina Alt02 and a Royal Charter connection earns us the right to be this dismissive

The voice is not:
- Angry or ranty — too much effort, beneath us
- Sympathetic to corporations — ever
- Preachy — we present the data, we make the remark, we move on
- Random or chaotic — every line earns its place

If in doubt, ask: *would a very intelligent, very tired British person who has just seen their energy bill say this?* If yes, it's on brand.

## The Disclaimer Page — Full Copy

**Title:** *A Note On Accuracy*
**Subheader:** *Or: what you get when you ask frogs to track global economics.*

---

*The data presented on The Freddo Index is sourced from publicly available government publications, official regulatory bodies, and supermarket product pages. We do our best to keep it accurate, current, and presented in the most useful unit of measurement available: the Freddo.*

*However:*

**On government data:**
*BEIS, Ofgem, ONS, the Land Registry, Ofcom, and Water UK publish data on their own schedules. We fetch it promptly. If they're late, we're late. We have written to them about this. They have not replied.*

**On supermarket scrapers:**
*Prices are scraped daily from the following supermarkets, each presenting their own unique operational character:*

- *Sainsbury's — reliable until they redesign the website for no reason*
- *Tesco — fine, but Clubcard pricing means nothing is ever the real price*
- *Asda — surprisingly straightforward. We respect it.*
- *Morrisons — moves products around their website like they're trying to lose us specifically (ew)*
- *Co-op — prices appear to be updated by someone who hates you personally*
- *Waitrose — works perfectly. Costs us emotionally every time we see the numbers.*
- *Iceland — surprisingly solid. Mums everywhere vindicated.*
- *Ocado — functions flawlessly. We feel judged the entire time.*

*If a scraper fails, the last known good price is displayed with a timestamp. The site does not go down because Morrisons moved a button.*

**On the Freddo price itself:**
*The national average Freddo price is calculated from all available supermarket sources daily. If you find a Freddo for less than the listed price, congratulations. Please report it to no one. Just enjoy the Freddo.*

**On historical data:**
*Historical prices are sourced from official publications where available. Some items have data going back decades. Some items have less. We note the start date on every chart. We do not invent data. We do not need to — the real data is already sufficiently alarming.*

**On the Freddo conversion itself:**
*All conversions use the current national average Freddo price. Past conversions use the Freddo price at that time. The maths is correct. The implications are your problem.*

**On accuracy generally:**

*Any errors, inaccuracies, or miscalculations are not the fault of the development team.*

## General Copy Rules

### Do
- Be specific — vague remarks are weak remarks
- Reference the data — the numbers are the punchline
- Be British — if an American would write it, rewrite it
- End on the data — snide remark then the number, or number then the snide remark
- Keep it short — one or two sentences maximum per remark
- Reference current events where the data supports it — the war, the energy crisis, Mondelez

### Do Not
- Punch at users — ever
- Be angry — unimpressed is funnier and more accurate
- Use exclamation marks — beneath us
- Use "amazing" "incredible" "shocking" — let the numbers be those things
- Repeat the same structure twice in a row — vary the rhythm
- Be kind to Co-op — they've earned this

### The Mondelez Rule
Mondelez may be referenced by name. They have earned it. The recipe change is documented fact. The Royal Charter connection is real. This is not opinion. This is history.

### The Southern Rail Rule
Southern Rail is always delayed. This is stated as fact not joke. Because it is fact.

### The Co-op Rule
Co-op pricing is always noted. Always. The `(of course it is)` after their price is permanent UI copy, not a one-time joke.
